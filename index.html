A working research method

Build a company case from claims and filings.

Start with the question and assigned reading. Then find the filings, pull the relevant facts, test the claim, and cite the source. The middle steps are working tools, so another reader can inspect the same evidence.

The pipeline

Inputs

Start with the assigned chapter and rubric. For team work, write down the agreed scope before drafting.

0

Scope the deliverable in scope

Decide exactly what the section has to answer. Here: two to four lessons other firms can copy. Out of that falls a skeleton of claims, no numbers yet.

1

Mine the primary text in scope

Pull the raw claim for each lesson straight from the chapter. This is the spine that matches the assignment, and most of the time it is enough on its own.

!

The fork: stop, or verify with numbers?

After reading the text, decide whether the assignment calls for filing-level verification. A book discussion and a financial case require different evidence.

Stay in scopeWrite the chapter-only version and answer the assigned question directly.
Go deeper (out of scope)Verify every claim against the filings and chart it. Rigorous, and easy to over-build past what was asked.
2

Find the reports deeper

Locate the company's SEC filings: the 10-K (annual), 10-Q (quarterly), 8-K (events). The Report Finder does this live against EDGAR.

Open the Report Finder
3

Dissect the filing deeper

Pull the line items into a time series and read the instruments: margins and returns, capex intensity, the cash conversion cycle, plus the Altman, Beneish, Piotroski, and Sloan screens. The Workbench does this on thirty bundled companies or any filing you drop in.

Open the Workbench
4

Chart and synthesize deeper

Turn the verified series into exhibits. Then state the conclusion the evidence supports, including the limits and counterexamples.

5

Produce and source always

Lay it out and label the evidence. Distinguish claims from the chapter from facts verified in filings, and identify any outside estimate.

The case behind the tools

These tools were factored out of one assignment: the lessons section of an Amazon case (Gallaugher, Chapter 8). The chapter argues four transferable lessons, and the deep version verified each against Amazon's SEC filings. That verification is what the Report Finder and the Workbench let you reproduce for yourself.
